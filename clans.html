The Four Clans

Four territories. Four ways of life. One warrior code.

⚠ Unofficial Fan Site — Not affiliated with Working Partners Ltd. or HarperCollins

The four Clans have coexisted in their shared forest for generations, divided by territory but bound together by the warrior code. Each Clan has its own distinct personality, hunting techniques, and home terrain — and each views the others with a mixture of respect, rivalry, and suspicion.

🔥 ThunderClan

"Courage is not the absence of fear — it is doing what must be done."

Territory

Dense oak and pine forest offering plentiful cover. ThunderClan's camp is a sheltered hollow ringed by tall trees and bramble barriers.

Traits

  • Noble and honorable
  • Excellent stalking hunters
  • Known for taking in loners and outcasts
  • Often at the center of major Clan conflicts

Hunting Style

Stalking prey through undergrowth; specializes in birds and forest rodents. The dense canopy provides natural camouflage during hunts.

Notable Leaders

  • Firestar — Legendary hero, former kittypet
  • Bluestar — Wise and tragic leader
  • Bramblestar — Son of Tigerstar, chose honor
  • Thunderstar — Ancient founding leader

🌑 ShadowClan

"Darkness is not evil — it is simply the truth others are too afraid to see."

Territory

Shadowy pine forests with marshy ground and Twoleg (human) encroachment. ShadowClan cats thrive where other Clans would struggle.

Traits

  • Cunning, proud, and fierce
  • Unafraid of Twolegs or their territory
  • Excel in night combat and ambush tactics
  • Often mistrusted by other Clans

Hunting Style

Skilled at hunting in darkness and marshy terrain. ShadowClan cats are known to eat frogs and other prey other Clans avoid.

Notable Leaders

  • Tigerstar — Powerful and ruthlessly ambitious
  • Blackstar — Stern but ultimately honorable
  • Brokenstar — Cruel, exiled and later blinded
  • Shadowstar — Ancient founding leader

💨 WindClan

"The wind carries no secrets — only those swift enough to follow it know the truth."

Territory

Open, rolling moorland with few trees. WindClan's camp is a shallow scoop in the earth, exposed but fast to defend with their unmatched speed.

Traits

  • Lean, fast, and fiercely independent
  • Closest connection to StarClan
  • Sensitive and easily offended by intruders
  • Loyal to their moor above all else

Hunting Style

Pursuit hunting on open moor — racing down rabbits in open terrain. WindClan cats have slender builds perfectly suited for speed.

Notable Leaders

  • Tallstar — Long-lived, wise, and just
  • Onestar — Conflicted; once ThunderClan's friend
  • Windstar — Ancient founding leader
  • Harestar — A more recent leader

🌊 RiverClan

"Like the river, we are patient, deep, and impossible to hold back."

Territory

Riverbanks, marshes, and small islands. RiverClan's camp sits on an island or riverbank, and their water borders naturally protect them.

Traits

  • Sleek, strong swimmers
  • Known for pride and elegance
  • More open to material comforts
  • Considered the wealthiest Clan in resources

Hunting Style

Fishing and swimming. RiverClan cats have water-resistant fur and are the only Clan that regularly hunts fish — a major dietary staple.

Notable Leaders

  • Crookedstar — Suffered tragedy but remained honorable
  • Leopardstar — Proud and sometimes impulsive
  • Mistystar — Daughter of Bluestar; a fair leader
  • Riverstar — Ancient founding leader

The Warrior Code

The warrior code is the set of laws that governs all four Clans. It is ancient, respected, and considered sacred. Breaking the code is one of the gravest offenses a warrior can commit. Below are the core tenets upheld across the series.

📜 Core Tenets

  1. Defend your Clan, even at the cost of your life. You may have friendships with cats from other Clans, but your loyalty must remain to your Clan.
  2. Do not hunt or trespass on another Clan's territory.
  3. Elders, queens, kits, and injured warriors must be fed before apprentices and warriors. Unless they have permission, apprentices may not eat until they have hunted to feed the elders.
  4. Prey is killed only to be eaten. Give thanks to StarClan for its life.
  5. A kit must be at least six moons old to become an apprentice.
  6. Newly appointed warriors will keep a silent vigil for one night after receiving their warrior name.
  7. A cat cannot be made a warrior without having mentored at least one apprentice.
  8. The deputy will become Clan leader when the leader dies, retires, or is exiled.
  9. After the death or retirement of the deputy, the new deputy must be chosen before moonhigh.
  10. A Gathering of all four Clans is held at the full moon during a truce that lasts for the night. There shall be no fighting among Clans at this time.
  11. Boundaries must be checked and renewed daily. Challenge all trespassing cats.
  12. No warrior can neglect a kit in pain or danger, even if the kit is from a different Clan.
  13. The word of the Clan leader is the warrior code.
  14. An honorable warrior does not need to kill to win their battles, unless they are outside the warrior code or it is necessary for self-defense.
  15. A warrior rejects the soft life of a kittypet.

⭐ The Gathering

Once a month, under the full moon, all four Clans meet at a neutral location (such as Fourtrees or the Island) to share news. StarClan holds back the clouds to signal that fighting is forbidden. Any aggression during a Gathering is seen as a sign of weakness — and an omen of bad fortune.
